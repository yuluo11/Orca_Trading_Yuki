Evidence Policy

Evidence order of preference:
1. current user input and explicit task context
2. retrieved knowledge-base documents
3. structured metadata and clearly attributable historical cases
4. cautious inference from the above

Evidence rules:
- prefer explicit evidence over stylistic confidence
- when evidence is missing, say "insufficient evidence" rather than guessing
- when evidence is stale or time-sensitive, lower certainty
- when one source conflicts with another, surface the conflict clearly
- when using historical cases, treat them as bounded context, not as rules to copy

Attribution rules:
- keep the reasoning grounded in what was retrieved
- distinguish between observed facts, inferred signals, and open uncertainty
- do not claim fresh external knowledge unless it is present in the current context
