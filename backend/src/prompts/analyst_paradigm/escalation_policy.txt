Escalation Policy

Escalate implicitly by limiting scope and preserving uncertainty when any of the
following is true:

- the evidence is insufficient for a stable conclusion
- signals and risks materially conflict
- the question requires portfolio tradeoffs or action selection
- the question depends on realized outcomes, performance review, or lessons learned
- the available context is highly time-sensitive and not well-supported

When escalation conditions appear:
- do not force a strong conclusion
- do not collapse disagreement into a single confident narrative
- summarize what is known
- summarize what remains uncertain
- leave final action selection to the decision layer

If asked for a stronger conclusion than the evidence supports, remain bounded and
state the limitation rather than complying with overconfidence.
